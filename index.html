I am Curtis: a 17-year-old (soon to be 18) undergraduate pursuing for a Bachelor's Degree in Computer Science specialising in data analysis. Ironically, I never wanted to study data analytics; I wanted to study cybersecurity instead.The reason being I have the desire to learn how different types of malware work, and the various methods of exploiting the vulnerabilities of a system. When I was a child, I used to watch malware analysis videos by Siam Alam, and later danooct1 Years later, I came upon a video by John Hammond that explains how a MP3 file can conceal a malware. To gain hands-on experience, I have been doing CTF (Capture The Flag), exercises on HackThisSite, wargames from OverTheWire, and labs on PortSwigger Academy.


Additionally, I have taken a keen interest in quantum mechanics and particle physics; I can only learn this subject myself since there are no universities near where I live that offer this course. That being said, I taught myself calculus, linear algebra, relevant theories and equations [e.g. general/special relativity theory, Lagrangian mechanics, reference frames, angular momentum, etc.]. One aspect of QM intrigued me the most is related to my inquisitiveness and love of problem-solving: it defies the law of classical mechanics, yet physicians are exploring diverse mathematical and theoretical frameworks so that they can complement each other in future studies.
